Chapter One

When Milk No Longer Satisfies

There was a season when faith felt almost effortless. Everything fit neatly into place. Worship songs were well worn and familiar, their melodies predictable in the most comforting way. Scriptures came easily to mind, quoted with confidence and fluency. Prayer required little strain. Words flowed without resistance, shaped by habit and reinforced by years of repetition. On the surface, stability seemed present. Beneath routine, something restless stirred. A quiet unease lingered that could not be ignored. Doubt was absent. Rebellion was absent. Hunger remained. A deeper hunger than comfort could satisfy. A hunger that milk could no longer fill.

I remember sitting in a church service during that season, listening to a message that was sound, biblical, and encouraging. The atmosphere felt warm. Heads nodded in agreement. Pens moved steadily across notebooks. Amens rose at familiar moments. Outwardly, every sign pointed toward success. Internally, emptiness remained. Offense was absent. Disengagement was absent. Emptiness remained. Listening became painful as recognition settled within me. Familiar truths echoed once again. Repent. Believe. Trust God. Every truth remained essential. Every principle remained foundational. Repetition without progression had quietly slowed growth. My mind recognized truth while my spirit longed for something weightier.

That same week, faith was tested outside church walls. A misunderstanding surfaced unexpectedly with someone close to me and weaknesses I preferred to hide suddenly appeared. Impatience rose quickly. Retreat felt safer than resolution. Silence felt easier than honesty rooted in love. I prayed, asking God for peace and relief from tension. Discomfort remained. Rather than relief, one Scripture echoed repeatedly within my thoughts: Hebrews 5:12. For the time ye ought to be teachers.

At first, resistance surfaced. Exhaustion became an excuse. Hurt became justification. Remaining where I was seemed easier and required less effort. Milk allows room for those arguments. Milk comforts without confrontation. Meat refuses negotiation with excuses. Meat confronts places where people settle prematurely.

That night, the Lord spoke through a dream. I found myself seated at a long table overflowing with food. People filled the room and bowls of milk sat before each person. As my hand reached toward one bowl, another hand gently stopped me. Looking upward, I saw Jesus. Calmness rested across His expression. Rebuke never appeared. Explanation never followed. One sentence came: You cannot eat this anymore.

I woke with my heart racing. Clarity settled immediately. Condemnation was absent. Rejection was absent. Invitation remained. Milk sustained me while learning, healing, and fragility defined earlier seasons. Purpose had been fulfilled. Remaining there would now limit what God desired to form within me. Jesus was calling me toward responsibility, obedience, and maturity.

Resolve never arrived instantly. Change unfolded gradually. Prayer changed. Listening changed. Questions changed. Rather than asking God for comfort, I began asking Him for transformation. Rather than asking Him to remove pressure, I asked Him to teach me through pressure. Relief no longer became the primary request. Transformation became the pursuit. That shift marked the true beginning of growth. A deliberate step away from milk had begun.
