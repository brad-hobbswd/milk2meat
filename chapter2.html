Chapter Two

Chewing Truth When It Hurts

Meat takes time. Meat does not dissolve instantly or disappear with little effort. Meat demands attention, patience, and intention. Spiritually, this season marked a turning point for me. Skimming Scripture for reassurance alone was no longer enough. Passages began confronting habits, motives, and places I had learned to protect rather than surrender. James 1:22 became unavoidable. Be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only.

This deeper engagement with truth led directly into a test I had avoided for years. The Lord began speaking to me about a fractured relationship that remained unresolved. Prayers had been offered many times. Private words of forgiveness had been spoken. Silence had been justified as wisdom. Distance had been labeled peace. Yet the Lord revealed something painful. Forgiveness that never moves toward obedience remains incomplete. Healing that avoids action often becomes delay wearing spiritual language.

Fear surfaced immediately. Layers of questions followed. What if conversation ended badly. What if intentions became misunderstood. What if old wounds reopened and nothing changed. Every question tempted retreat. Milk would have accommodated those fears. Milk would have allowed avoidance to appear wise. Meat required obedience without guarantees.

One night after wrestling for hours in prayer, the Lord spoke through another dream. I saw myself walking down a narrow hallway. Walls felt close and heavy doors lined both sides. Every door carried a label. Offense. Pride. Fear. Control. Jesus stood waiting at the far end. No motion came. No instructions came. He simply waited. Understanding settled deeply within me. Reaching Him required passing places I had spent years avoiding. No alternate route existed. No shortcut existed. Obedience remained the only path.

The next day I chose action. I reached out. I showed up. Conversation felt awkward and uncomfortable. Reconciliation never arrived dramatically and immediate resolution never followed. Something more important happened internally. Strength formed within me. Emotional relief never arrived first. Spiritual firmness did. Obedience had been exercised.

That experience changed my understanding of maturity. Obedience becomes the muscle of spiritual growth. Strength develops through use. Discernment sharpens through action. Endurance forms through obedience. Meat does not necessarily make life easier, though meat strengthens believers for every season ahead.
